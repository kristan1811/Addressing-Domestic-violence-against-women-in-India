# Domestic Violence Risk Analysis in India

## Overview
This project analyzes domestic violence and crimes against women across Indian states by combining NFHS-5 socio-economic indicators with NCRB crime data. The goal is to identify key factors associated with domestic violence, uncover regional patterns, and classify states into risk categories using data analytics and machine learning.

## Objectives
- Analyze domestic violence patterns across Indian states
- Study the impact of literacy, education, child marriage, and teen pregnancy
- Examine relationships between alcohol/tobacco consumption and crime indicators
- Identify high-risk and low-risk states
- Segment states using clustering techniques
- Generate policy-relevant insights

## Datasets Used
### NFHS-5 (National Family Health Survey)
- Female Literacy
- Women with 10+ Years Schooling
- Child Marriage
- Teen Pregnancy
- Male Alcohol Consumption
- Male Tobacco Consumption

### NCRB 2022 Crime Data
- Cruelty by Husband/Relatives
- Dowry Deaths
- Crimes Against Women
- Domestic Violence Indicators

## Key Insights
- Higher literacy and educational attainment are generally associated with lower child marriage rates.
- Child marriage and teen pregnancy show strong relationships with domestic violence indicators.
- Alcohol consumption exhibits noticeable association with crimes against women.
- Significant regional disparities exist across Indian states.
- Clustering reveals distinct state-level risk profiles that can support targeted interventions.
